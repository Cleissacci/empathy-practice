01 — Brand Identity

Who is Lumina?

A psychological services company built on the belief that empathy is a skill — learnable, practicable, and transformative. Lumina designs experiences that bring clarity and warmth to the most human of all capabilities.

Brand Voice
Warm
We speak as a trusted colleague — never clinical, never cold. We make space before we make a point.
Grounded
Evidence-based language. No wellness buzzwords. We earn credibility through precision.
Inviting
Psychological safety first. We design interfaces that feel safe to explore, safe to be wrong in.
Clear
We say what we mean in plain language. Active voice. Short sentences. Room to breathe.
Signature Element — The Breath Wave
The breath wave is our signature motif — a sine wave evoking mindful breathing, active listening, and the natural rhythm of empathic exchange. It appears in dividers, loading states, illustrations, and course progress indicators.

10 — Alerts & Feedback

Feedback Patterns

Feedback messages are warm and directive — never alarming. Errors explain what happened and what to do next. Successes reinforce growth without over-celebrating.

13 — Usage Guidelines

Design Principles

These are the rules we hold to — in every screen, every state, every piece of copy. They keep Lumina feeling like itself no matter who's building.

Do
✓Use Cormorant Garamond for all headings, module titles, and display text
✓Use warm ivory (#F7F4F0) as the base background — never pure white (#FFFFFF) on its own as a page background
✓Write error messages that explain what happened and what to do next
✓Use SVG icons from Lucide — always at 24×24px viewBox with 2px stroke
✓Apply a minimum 44×44px touch target to all interactive elements
✓Add visible focus rings on all interactive elements for keyboard navigation
✓Keep body text between 65–72 characters per line for optimal readability
✓Use soft, warm-tinted shadows — the drop shadow values in --shadow-* are calibrated for this
✓Disable buttons during async operations to prevent double-submission
✓Respect prefers-reduced-motion for all transitions and animations
Don't
✗Use emojis as interface icons — they are not accessible and break visual consistency
✗Hardcode hex values in component styles — always use a CSS token
✗Use amber (#C4956A) for normal-size body text on white — it doesn't meet 4.5:1
✗Use Nunito for large display text — it loses gravitas at 36px+; that's Cormorant's territory
✗Add box-shadow: 0 0 20px rgba(0,0,0,0.4) — this feels clinical and alarming
✗Use border-radius: 0 anywhere — every element should feel soft and approachable
✗Write alerts that only say "An error occurred" — be specific and compassionate
✗Stack z-index values without using the defined scale (--z-base, --z-overlay, etc.)
✗Use color alone to communicate status — always pair with an icon or text label
✗Use transitions longer than 300ms on UI interactions — they feel sluggish and anxious
